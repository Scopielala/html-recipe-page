Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is more than just a meal, it is a cultural icon and point of pride accross Nigeria. At it's core Nigerian Jollof is made with long-grain parboiled rice cooked in a deeply seasoned tomato-sauce. The stew or pepper mix, is a blend of tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, and spices, all cooked down until thick and intensely flavorful. This mixture gives jollof its signature taste: slightly sweet from the tomatoes, spicy from the peppers and savory from the seasoning.

What sets Nigerian Jollof apart is the cooking technique. The rice is simmered directly in the sauce rather than being cooked separately. Traditionally, it is cooked over firewood at parties, which gives it a distinct smoky aroma, that smoky depth is highly prized and difficult to replicate perfectly on a standard stove. Jollof carries social significance. it's at the center of friendly rivalry, especially the famous "Jollof Wars" between Nigeria and Ghana, each claiming superiority in flavor and technique

Recipe Details

Prep Time Cook Time Servings Difficulty
20 minutes 55 minutes 6 - 8 people Medium

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice, washed and drained
  • 6 medium ripe plum tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 3 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 1 large white onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato puree
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 11/2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend the pepper base: Add the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and the blended half of the onion into a blender. Blend until smooth and set aside.
  2. Fry down the sauce: Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Fry the sliced onion until golden, then pour in the blended pepper and tomato puree. Cook uncovered for 25 - 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the water dries out and the oil floats on top.
  3. Season the sauce: Add the seasoning cubes, curry powder, thyme, smoked paprika, white pepper, bay leaves, and salt. Stir well and taste - the sauce should be boldly seasoned.
  4. Add rice and stock: Pour in the washed rice and stir to coat every grain. Add the stock, the liquid should just barely cover the rice. Top up with a little water if needed.
  5. Seal and steam: Cover the pot tightly with aluminium foil, then place the lid on top. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting and cook for 25 - 30 minutes without lifting the lid.
  6. Check and finish: After 25 minutes, check that the rice is cooked through and the sauce is fully absorbed. For the classic smoky "party jollof" bottom, allow the pot to sit on high heat for a final 3 - 5 minutes.
  7. Serve: Discard the bay leaves, fluff the rice with a fork, and serve hot alongside fried chicken, plantain, or coleslaw.

Tips & Notes

  • Always use parboiled long-grain rice, it holds its shape and absorbs flavour well.
  • Do not rush the frying of the tomato base; this step builds the depth of flavor.
  • Sealing the pot with foil before the lid traps steam and ensures the rice cooks evenly.
  • Leftover Jollof Rice keeps in the fridge for up to 4 days and reheats well with a splash of water.
  • To make it vegan, swap children stock for vegetable stock.
